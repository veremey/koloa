OUR PRODUCTION & DISTILLING PROCESS

Ever since our first batch of rum was bottled and distilled in September 2009, the Kōloa Rum Company has been bringing the Spirit of Aloha to the world.

To produce Kōloa Rum, we use pristine rainwater from Mount Waiʻaleʻale and the nearby mountain peaks and rainforests that filtered through volcanic strata into vast underground aquifers. Our rums only use pure Hawaiian “tall cane” sugarcane, no molasses, and locally-sourced coffee, coconut and pineapple to develop authentic flavors of the spirits we produce.

Using these unique, authentic ingredients, our premium Hawaiian rums are distilled, produced and bottled at our distillery near the town of Kalaheo, on the south side of the beautiful Garden Isle of Kaua’i. Our rums are distilled twice using a vintage 1,210-gallon copper-pot still with a copper column and condenser.

It is the distillation process and unique, authentic ingredients that impart a remarkable smoothness and rich aroma not found in typical rums.

Our distillery also supports production of Hawaiian Fruit Specialties and its Kukui Brand, which provides cocktail mixes, jams, jellies and syrups to enjoy alongside Kōloa Rum products. Founded in 1931 by the Tateishi family, the company has focused on utilizing the natural, abundant resources of Kaua’i. We are proud to perpetuate the recipes and long family history of hard work and dedication that the Tateishis began.
